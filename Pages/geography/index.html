Medicine Hat is divided into three parts, once by the river valley carved by the South Saskatchewan River, and twice by Cottonwood Coulee that runs from the southwest boundary to the southeast end of town. The northern side of the river is Crescent Heights, a neighbourhood situated on the highest spot in the city, constructed across the ridge overlooking the river valley.
The zone on the opposite side of the river is named River Heights, which stretches from the trans Canada highway to the west, to the eastern boundary of downtown. River Heights is home to the local hospital and merges with the neighbourhoods of Southwest Hill and Southeast Hill, the latter bordering Cottonwood Coulee.



The Seven Persons Creek, which snakes its way through the prairie landscape for eight kilometres before trickling into city limits, runs through the center of the coulee, and many hiking trails allow exploration alongside the babbling brook and into the tangled cottonwood groves surrounding it.
If you’re looking for a desert like climate, but don’t want to fully commit to year long heat, Medicine Hat is the perfect place for you! Often referred to as “the sunniest city in Canada”, Medicine Hat gets an average of 300 days of sunshine, so a cloudy day is quite rare. Medicine Hat gets so little precipitation, it is technically a desert, but it is still subject to the typical Canadian prairie weather patterns; hot summers, cold winters, and unpredictable springs and autumns. In this city, you can get hot sunny days without giving up white Christmases.
